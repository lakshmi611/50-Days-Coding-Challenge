# 📊 Employee Stress Level Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee stress levels using survey and activity data to identify key factors affecting workplace well-being. The goal is to provide actionable insights that help organizations improve employee productivity and mental health.


## 🎯 Objective

* Analyze employee stress patterns

* Identify high-risk employees

* Understand impact of work hours, sleep, and lifestyle

* Provide data-driven recommendations for better workplace policies


## 🗂️ Dataset Description

The dataset includes the following fields:

* **EmployeeID** – Unique identifier

* **Department** – Employee department

* **Age** – Employee age

* **Gender** – Male/Female

* **WorkHours** – Average daily working hours

* **SleepHours** – Average daily sleep hours

* **ExerciseHours** – Weekly exercise hours

* **CaffeineIntake** – Daily caffeine consumption

* **StressLevel** – Stress rating (1 = Low, 5 = Very High)

* **DateRecorded** – Date of record


## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

* **Power BI** – Data visualization & dashboard

* **DAX** – Calculations & measures

* **Power Query** – Data cleaning and transformation


## 📊 Key KPIs

* Average Stress Level

* High Stress Percentage (StressLevel ≥ 4)

* Average Sleep Hours

* Average Work Hours

* Sleep-to-Work Ratio


## 📈 Dashboard Features

* 📊 **Column Chart** – Stress Level by Department

* 📈 **Line Chart** – Stress Trend Over Time

* ⚫ **Scatter Plot** – Work Hours vs Stress Level

* 🍩 **Donut Chart** – Stress Distribution

* ⚠️ **Conditional Formatting Table** – Highlights high-risk employees

* 🎛️ **Slicers** – Department, Gender, Age Group


## 🚨 Conditional Formatting Logic

Employees are flagged as **High Risk** if:

* Stress Level = 5

* Sleep Hours < 5

This helps quickly identify employees needing attention.


## 🔍 Key Insights

* Employees working longer hours tend to have higher stress levels

* Low sleep duration (<6 hours) is strongly linked to high stress

* Certain departments consistently show higher stress patterns

* Exercise has a moderate impact on reducing stress

* High caffeine intake is common among stressed employees


## 💡 Recommendations

* Encourage work-life balance by limiting excessive work hours

* Promote healthy sleep habits among employees

* Introduce wellness and fitness programs

* Monitor high-risk employees regularly

* Reduce dependency on caffeine through awareness programs
